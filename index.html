Range Card Builder — Instructions

A point-mass ballistics solver that builds printable DOPE and BDC reticle cards. Fill in the panels on the left; the cards update live. Everything runs in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

Getting Started

Pick your Caliber, then choose a Factory load preset to auto-fill bullet weight, ballistic coefficient (BC), drag model, and muzzle velocity. Adjust anything by hand afterward. Set your Optic details and Zero range, then read the DOPE card on the right.

Most important input: muzzle velocity. Factory numbers come from a 24" test barrel — your rifle will differ. Chronograph when you can, or use True Your Dope (below) to back it out from real impacts.

Rifle & Ammunition

Caliber selects which factory-load presets appear. Bullet weight, BC, and muzzle velocity drive the trajectory. Drag model (G1 or G7): use whichever the manufacturer publishes. G1 suits flat-base and shorter bullets; G7 tracks long boat-tail match bullets better past ~400 yd. Never mix them — a G7 number entered as G1 will wildly overstate drop.

Optic

Height over bore (HOB) is the distance from the center of the scope to the center of the bore, in inches — measure it, as it affects close-range holds and BDC circle ranges. Zero range is where your rifle is sighted dead-on. Turret units (MIL or MOA) sets what the elevation and wind columns display; match it to your scope's turrets. Click value sets the per-click increment for the CLICKS column.

Atmosphere & Wind

Temperature, humidity, and either altitude or station pressure set the air density the bullet flies through. Use station pressure if you have a Kestrel or weather-app reading (it already accounts for elevation); otherwise use altitude. Incline is the up/down shot angle in degrees — leave at 0 for level ground. Wind speed and Wind from (clock direction: 3 or 9 o'clock is full-value crosswind, 12 or 6 is none) drive the wind hold columns.

True Your Dope

The most valuable feature. Shoot a group at a known distance, enter that range and the actual elevation that centered your group, choose whether to solve muzzle velocity or BC, and press True It. The tool back-solves the value that makes prediction match reality, then updates every card.

Solve muzzle velocity for most error (it's usually the culprit). Solve BC only when your close-range dope matches but far-range is off. True at 300+ yd where error is large enough to see.

Max Point Blank Range (MPBR)

Enter your target's vital zone size in inches (≈3–4" for prairie dogs, 8" for coyote/deer vitals) and press Compute. The tool finds the zero that keeps the bullet inside that zone as far as possible, and reports the resulting zero, the 100-yd sight-in height, and the max point blank range — the farthest you can hold dead center without missing high or low. Use This Zero applies it to your setup.

Card Range & Trajectory Graph

Start / End / Step set the distances shown on the DOPE card. Time of flight and Trajectory graph are optional toggles — the graph plots bullet path vs. line of sight with your zero and max ordinate marked.

BDC / Reticle Card

This card does the inverse of the DOPE card: instead of "range → hold," it tells you what range each reticle holdover mark actually hits for your load. Two reticle types:

Even spacing — for mil-hash or MOA-hash reticles with evenly spaced marks. Set the mark spacing (e.g. 0.5 mil) and number of marks.

Custom marks (BDC circles) — for factory BDC reticles (like Nikon) whose circles are unevenly spaced. Enter each circle's subtension, comma-separated, in your current turret unit. Read the true subtensions from the manufacturer's ballistic program (e.g. Nikon Spot On's reticle-view mode) for best accuracy.

Focal plane: First (FFP) reticles are true at any magnification — nothing more to set. Second (SFP) reticles are only true at one calibrated power; set Reticle true at (usually max magnification) and Current mag (what you're dialed to), and the card scales the holds accordingly. The current magnification prints in the card title so multiple cards don't get confused.

Subtension is an angular value, so it must be entered in the same unit (MIL or MOA) the tool is set to. The same circle is 0.53 mil or 1.82 MOA — entering the wrong unit's number will be badly off.

Saving, Printing & Sharing

Profiles save a complete setup (rifle, load, optic, conditions, BDC config) in your browser so you can recall it instantly — one profile per rifle/load. Export / Import JSON moves profiles between devices, since they're stored per-browser. DOPE PNG and BDC PNG download card images; Print outputs all visible cards.

The Bottom Line

Every computed number is a starting estimate built on your inputs. Real impacts on paper always win. Confirm at distance, record what the rifle actually does in the ACTUAL / NOTES column, and true the card to your rifle, your load lot, your air, and your scope.
